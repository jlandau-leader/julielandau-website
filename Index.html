The reality you're in

You might know
this feeling.

  • You can't remember the last time you felt like yourself in a meeting — not performing, not managing, just present.
  • You're making the biggest calls of your career and you've never felt less sure of the person making them.
  • You used to move from fire. Now you mostly move from obligation.

None of it means you're failing.
It means you've outgrown the identity that got you here.

Maybe you haven't named it yet.

You just know the version of you that got here has started to feel like a costume.

You're successful enough to know that more success won't fix it.

The real shift

From performing it
to living it.

Most leaders I work with aren't struggling with capability.

They are struggling with the gap between what they project and what they feel.

Performing wellbeing — and exhausted.

Performing leadership — and feeling completely alone at the top.

Performance is expensive. It costs your energy, your presence, and eventually your body.

The shift is not a better performance.

Biography

Julie Landau

My father died when I was six. I started riding horses that same year. You cannot dominate a horse — you listen, attune, and lead from presence. Every leadership lesson I carry today was learned in that arena first.

I moved to Shanghai with one suitcase and no job waiting. Seventeen years leading teams across Asia followed — building P&Ls, scaling operations, VP at Alibaba. The weight you're carrying is not theory to me. I have carried it.

What the résumé doesn't say: I rebuilt myself from the ground up. Recovered alcoholic. Recovered from bulimia. I have restructured not just companies but the deepest parts of who I am.

Two coaching sessions in a Buddhist temple in Thailand changed my direction entirely. I flew to London on one week's notice — only a gut yes so clear it wasn't optional.

That is how I make decisions now. And it is what I will teach you.

A few honest questions

What leaders ask
before we begin.

A consultant hands you a strategy — which creates dependency on their expertise. I develop your own capacity to see clearly and decide from your own ground. I blend both: identity-level coaching from someone who has led real P&Ls across three continents, so the work meets the real complexity on your desk while the clarity and authority stay entirely yours.

It moves your performance precisely because it works on the real bottleneck at your level — the state you lead from. Every framework you've tried, every programme, every book — none of them changed the underlying identity that determines how you actually show up. This work does. The results are not soft. They are the hardest, highest-leverage work available to a leader at your level.

Because understanding and embodying are not the same thing — and at senior levels, almost no one tells you that. You can know exactly what kind of leader you want to be and still, under real pressure, the old version shows up. That gap is not a knowledge problem. Not a discipline problem. It is an identity problem. Most leadership development produces change you have to consciously maintain — which is just a more sophisticated form of performance. This work goes underneath that. When the identity shifts, the behaviour follows without effort.

Yes — and it's almost universally misunderstood. It's not weakness. It's a signal that your role has outpaced the identity that earned it. The confidence didn't disappear — it's buried under conditioning built for your previous level. We don't build new confidence. We surface the leader who was already there, underneath everything that got layered on top.

Burnout at senior levels is rarely a time-management problem. It is the cost of an identity that makes rest feel like a threat and delegation feel like losing control. When that shifts, you stop operating from survival. You don't do less — you stop leaking energy into a performance that was never yours.

Most coaching works at the level of behaviour and insight. This works at the level of identity. Insight without a shift in who you're being produces temporary change at best. If you've found yourself understanding exactly what you need to do differently and still not doing it — that gap is precisely what this work addresses.

You're ready if you've earned real success and can feel — not just intellectually, but in your body — that there is another level available. If you're willing to look at yourself as the primary variable. If you want a shift, not more frameworks. The work is personal, direct, and held with genuine care. It is not for everyone. That's exactly why it works for the people it's for.
